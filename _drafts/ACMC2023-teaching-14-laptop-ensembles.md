# Teaching music from computing since 2018

* Started for-credit “laptop ensemble” in computing in 2018 as a  _special topic course_
* At  __ACMC2019__  argued the case for bringing CS and music students together in LENS:
  * Bring “creativity” to CS education
  * Emphasise “tool creation” in music tech

Can we scale the LENS experience to meet demands of large student numbers; in other words,  __can we run a LENS class with 50 students?__  What about 500?

# What wasn’t working?

Good students  __didn’t reach potential 😨__

Didn’t know how to collaborate

Didn’t know how to improvise

Didn’t get far enough to apply advanced computer music concepts in final concert.

# The change: beat cyphers

* Also at ACMC2019: Benjamin Murphy discussed a “beat cypher” approach to song production at Box Hill Tafe.
  * __Insight: __ students have produced few songs and need to make  _lots_  to gain skills
  * __Class:__  Make a song a week following a prompt. Listen and discuss in class.
* __OUCH!__  My class is an anti-pattern! They won’t learn by making one piece!

# Workshops

Weekly 2-hour  _[session](https://comp.anu.edu.au/courses/laptop-ensemble/workshops/)_  with a small group (15-20)

__At home: __ create a 1min piece (every week), record screen

__In class: __ watch performance, share knowledge, live improv, discuss

__By the end:__  8 solo pieces, 2 group pieces, 1 final performance.

| __time__  | __activity__  |
| :-: | :-: |
| 00:00 | show up, get settled, ask any pre-class questions |
| 00:10 | listen to that week’s CM diary submissions |
| 00:30 | crit discussion |
| 00:50 | small group jam activity |
| 01:20 | group performances |
| 01:40 | discussion, feedback, questions on notice |

# Insights

Students create 11 pieces over the semester, not 1.

Improv starts in  __week 1__ .

Students need to  _learn_  to collaborate (have crises early, not the day before concert).

Needed workflow to watch all the videos at once, then discuss (more on this later).

Peer-learning and student-led  process can be  _transcendent_  when students show up for each other.

# Ensembles

Double down on ensemble creation and development as the  __main activity__  of the class.

Ensembles created in week 4.

Ensembles create group diaries for week 5 and 9.

__Hope:__ shake out social problems early.

__Goal:__  balance ensemble (micro) with workshop (meso) and lecture (macro)

We had 14 ensembles across three workshops!

# Video Tasks

Video: an “alternative” assessment medium.

Where are the LMS tools for handling video at scale?

We created a tool chain for automatically editing/combining workshop videos.

Python code knows students / week / folder structure.

Runs FFMPEG to generate weekly videos (60 individual students in 3 workshop groups).

Bonus feature: normalises audio, adds student name as an overlay.

# What do computing students get out of SMC?

Just  __“creativity”__  is not enough.

Primarily teaching to upper UG/Master computing students.

Apply  __integrated system knowledge__ : coding, OS, networks, interfaces, interaction, design.

Collaboration and social skills.

Music and improv (good for human spirit, but what else?)

Lots of courses teach some of these!
