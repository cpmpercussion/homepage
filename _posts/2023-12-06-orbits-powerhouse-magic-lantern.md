In August 2023, I performed live electronic music as part of a magic lantern show at the [Powerhouse Museum](https://powerhouse.com.au) in Ultimo, Sydney. 
We were invited to present the show as a response to the museum's drawing exhibition *A Line, A Web, A World*. The recording of my live soundtrack performance became the album [*Orbits*](https://charlesmartin.bandcamp.com/album/orbits).

![Mobile music setup at the Powerhouse Museum, Sydney]({% link assets/blog/2023/202308-lanterns-powerhouse-1.jpg %})

The slide show was created by [Martyn Jolly](https://martynjolly.com) with a mix of vintage hand-coloured, painted, and mechanical slides from the 1880s–1900s and contemporary inkjet slides made from Anna Madeleine Raupach's works *Signal to Noise* and *Light Echoes/Standard Stars* (2023).

We developed a show that takes the audience from pastoral scenes, to the ocean, the sky, and then into outer space before, heading deep into the sun for the end of the show.
I performed with field recordings, digital synthesizers, and touch-pad instruments. With a techno and ambient inspired set reflecting the dramatic movement of the show.

Unusually, we performed the show three times in one night with different audiences in the small theatre. Hard work, but I really enjoy the chance to refine a set like this, improving and experimenting over multiple shows.

![The Kings Theatre within the Powerhouse was our venue.]({% link assets/blog/2023/202308-lanterns-powerhouse-2.jpg %})

## Orbits

The recording of the performance is now available as the album *Orbits* on Bandcamp. The development and practice behind this set and the repeated performances led to a product that I felt worked particularly well.
